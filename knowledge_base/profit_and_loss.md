# Profit & Loss Statement (Income Statement)

## What is a Profit & Loss Statement?
A Profit & Loss (P&L) statement, also called an Income Statement, is a financial report summarizing revenues, costs, and expenses during a specific period (monthly, quarterly, or annually). It shows whether a business made a profit or incurred a loss.

## The P&L Formula
**Net Profit (or Loss) = Total Revenue − Total Expenses**

## Structure of a P&L Statement

### 1. Revenue (Income)
All money earned from business operations:
- Sales Revenue
- Service Income
- Interest Income
- Other Income

### 2. Cost of Goods Sold (COGS)
Direct costs of producing goods or services:
- Raw Materials
- Direct Labor
- Manufacturing Overhead

**Gross Profit = Revenue − COGS**

### 3. Operating Expenses
Indirect costs of running the business:
- Salaries and Wages
- Rent
- Utilities
- Marketing and Advertising
- Depreciation
- Office Supplies
- Insurance

**Operating Profit (EBIT) = Gross Profit − Operating Expenses**

### 4. Non-Operating Items
- Interest Expense (on loans)
- Tax Expense
- Other non-operational income/losses

**Net Profit = Operating Profit − Interest − Taxes**

## Key Metrics from P&L

### Gross Profit Margin
(Gross Profit / Revenue) × 100
Measures efficiency of production.

### Net Profit Margin
(Net Profit / Revenue) × 100
Measures overall profitability.

### Operating Expense Ratio
(Operating Expenses / Revenue) × 100

## Importance of P&L
- Evaluate business performance over time
- Identify areas of high spending
- Make decisions about pricing and cost control
- Required for tax filing and investor reporting
- Track trends in revenue and expenses
