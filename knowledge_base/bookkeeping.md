# Bookkeeping

## What is Bookkeeping?
Bookkeeping is the systematic recording of all financial transactions in a business. It is the foundation of accounting and ensures that financial records are accurate, up-to-date, and complete.

## Key Concepts

### Double-Entry Bookkeeping
Every financial transaction affects at least two accounts. For every debit entry, there must be a corresponding credit entry of equal value. This system keeps the accounting equation balanced:
**Assets = Liabilities + Equity**

### Single-Entry Bookkeeping
A simpler method where each transaction is recorded once. Suitable for very small businesses or sole proprietors with minimal transactions.

## Common Bookkeeping Tasks
- Recording sales and purchases
- Managing accounts payable and receivable
- Reconciling bank statements
- Tracking expenses and income
- Maintaining the general ledger
- Payroll processing

## The General Ledger
The general ledger is the master record of all financial transactions. It contains all accounts used by a business and serves as the basis for financial statements.

## Chart of Accounts
A chart of accounts is an organized list of all accounts in the general ledger. Common categories include:
- **Assets**: Cash, Accounts Receivable, Inventory, Equipment
- **Liabilities**: Accounts Payable, Loans, Credit Card Debt
- **Equity**: Owner's Capital, Retained Earnings
- **Revenue**: Sales, Service Income
- **Expenses**: Rent, Utilities, Salaries, Supplies

## Best Practices
1. Record transactions daily or weekly
2. Reconcile bank accounts monthly
3. Keep personal and business finances separate
4. Maintain digital backups of all records
5. Use consistent categorization for expenses
