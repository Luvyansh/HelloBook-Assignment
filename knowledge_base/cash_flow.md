# Cash Flow Statement

## What is a Cash Flow Statement?
A cash flow statement is a financial report that shows how cash moves in and out of a business over a specific period. Unlike the P&L, it focuses purely on actual cash movements — not accrued income or expenses.

## Why Cash Flow Matters
A business can be profitable on paper but still fail due to poor cash flow. Cash flow management ensures the business can pay its bills, employees, and invest in growth.

**"Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity, but cash flow is reality."**

## The Three Sections of Cash Flow

### 1. Operating Activities
Cash generated or used from core business operations.

**Cash Inflows:**
- Collections from customers
- Interest and dividend income received

**Cash Outflows:**
- Payments to suppliers
- Employee salaries
- Rent and utilities
- Taxes paid
- Interest paid

**Net Cash from Operations = Cash Inflows − Cash Outflows (Operating)**

### 2. Investing Activities
Cash used for or generated from investments in assets.

**Cash Inflows:**
- Sale of property, plant, or equipment
- Sale of investments or securities
- Collection of loans made to others

**Cash Outflows:**
- Purchase of PP&E (Capital Expenditures / CapEx)
- Purchase of investments
- Loans made to others

### 3. Financing Activities
Cash related to funding the business.

**Cash Inflows:**
- Proceeds from issuing stock
- Borrowings (loans, bonds)

**Cash Outflows:**
- Repayment of loans
- Dividend payments
- Share buybacks

## Net Change in Cash
**Net Change = Operating + Investing + Financing Cash Flows**
**Ending Cash = Beginning Cash + Net Change**

## Methods of Preparing Cash Flow Statement

### Direct Method
Lists actual cash receipts and payments. More transparent but time-consuming.

### Indirect Method
Starts with net profit and adjusts for non-cash items and working capital changes. More commonly used.

## Key Cash Flow Metrics

### Free Cash Flow (FCF)
Operating Cash Flow − Capital Expenditures
Represents cash available after maintaining/expanding assets.

### Operating Cash Flow Ratio
Operating Cash Flow / Current Liabilities
Measures ability to pay short-term obligations.

### Cash Burn Rate
How quickly a business uses its cash reserves (important for startups).

## Importance of Cash Flow Statement
- Reveals actual liquidity position
- Helps predict future cash needs
- Required by investors and lenders
- Complements P&L and Balance Sheet
- Essential for budgeting and forecasting
