# Accounts Payable & Accounts Receivable

## Accounts Receivable (AR)

### What is Accounts Receivable?
Accounts Receivable represents money owed to your business by customers who have received goods or services but have not yet paid. It is recorded as a current asset on the balance sheet.

### AR Process
1. Sell goods/services on credit
2. Issue invoice to customer
3. Record as accounts receivable
4. Follow up on payment
5. Record payment when received
6. Remove from AR, add to cash

### Managing Accounts Receivable
- Set clear payment terms (Net 30, Net 60)
- Send invoices promptly
- Follow up on overdue invoices
- Offer early payment discounts
- Use aging reports to track outstanding invoices

### AR Aging Report
Categorizes outstanding invoices by how long they've been unpaid:
- 0–30 days
- 31–60 days
- 61–90 days
- 90+ days (high risk of bad debt)

### Bad Debt
When AR is deemed uncollectible, it becomes a bad debt expense. Businesses may create an "allowance for doubtful accounts" to estimate expected losses.

## Accounts Payable (AP)

### What is Accounts Payable?
Accounts Payable represents money your business owes to suppliers and vendors for goods or services received but not yet paid for. It is recorded as a current liability on the balance sheet.

### AP Process
1. Receive goods or services from vendor
2. Receive vendor invoice
3. Record as accounts payable
4. Approve invoice for payment
5. Make payment by due date
6. Remove from AP, reduce cash

### Managing Accounts Payable
- Review invoices carefully before approval
- Take advantage of early payment discounts
- Avoid late payment penalties
- Maintain good supplier relationships
- Reconcile AP regularly with vendor statements

### AP Controls
- Three-way matching: Purchase Order + Goods Receipt + Invoice
- Segregation of duties (different people for approval and payment)
- Regular AP aging review

## AR vs AP Summary

| Feature | Accounts Receivable | Accounts Payable |
|---|---|---|
| Definition | Money owed TO you | Money you OWE |
| Balance Sheet | Current Asset | Current Liability |
| Created by | Selling on credit | Buying on credit |
| Goal | Collect quickly | Pay strategically |
