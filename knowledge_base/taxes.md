# Taxes in Business Accounting

## Types of Business Taxes

### Income Tax
Tax levied on a business's net profit. The rate depends on business structure (sole proprietor, partnership, corporation) and jurisdiction.

### GST / VAT (Goods and Services Tax / Value Added Tax)
An indirect consumption tax applied at each stage of the supply chain.
- Businesses collect GST from customers (Output Tax)
- Businesses pay GST to suppliers (Input Tax)
- **Net GST Payable = Output Tax − Input Tax**

### Payroll Tax
Tax related to employee compensation, including:
- Income tax withholding (TDS)
- Social security contributions
- Employer contributions (PF, ESI in India)

### Corporate Tax
Tax on a corporation's profits, filed annually.

## Tax-Related Accounting Concepts

### Tax Deductible Expenses
Business expenses that reduce taxable income:
- Salaries and wages
- Rent for business premises
- Depreciation on assets
- Business travel
- Professional fees (legal, accounting)
- Marketing and advertising

### Depreciation (for Tax Purposes)
Assets lose value over time. Depreciation is deducted as an expense to reduce taxable income.
- **Straight-Line Method**: Equal depreciation each year
- **Declining Balance Method**: Higher depreciation in early years

### Deferred Tax
Temporary differences between accounting profit and taxable profit create deferred tax assets or liabilities.

## Tax Compliance

### Record Keeping Requirements
- Keep all invoices, receipts, and financial records
- Retain records for minimum 7 years (varies by jurisdiction)
- Maintain separate business bank accounts

### Filing Deadlines
- Quarterly GST/VAT returns
- Annual income tax returns
- Monthly payroll tax deposits

## Tax Planning vs Tax Evasion
- **Tax Planning**: Legal use of tax laws to minimize liability
- **Tax Avoidance**: Using loopholes — legally gray area
- **Tax Evasion**: Illegal concealment of income — criminal offense

## Chart of Accounts: Tax-Related Accounts
- GST/VAT Payable (Liability)

- Income Tax Payable (Liability)
