# Invoices

## What is an Invoice?
An invoice is a commercial document issued by a seller to a buyer indicating the products or services provided, quantities, agreed prices, and payment terms. It serves as a formal request for payment.

## Types of Invoices

### Standard Invoice
The most common type, used for one-time sales of goods or services.

### Proforma Invoice
A preliminary invoice sent before goods or services are delivered. It outlines the estimated costs and is not a demand for payment.

### Recurring Invoice
Used for ongoing services billed on a regular schedule (monthly, quarterly, etc.).

### Credit Invoice (Credit Note)
Issued to reduce the amount owed by the buyer, typically for returns or billing errors.

### Debit Invoice (Debit Note)
Issued to increase the amount owed, often due to under-billing or additional charges.

## Key Components of an Invoice
1. **Invoice Number**: Unique identifier for tracking
2. **Invoice Date**: When the invoice was issued
3. **Due Date**: When payment is expected
4. **Seller Information**: Name, address, contact, tax ID
5. **Buyer Information**: Name, address, contact
6. **Itemized List**: Description, quantity, unit price, subtotal
7. **Subtotal**: Total before taxes/discounts
8. **Taxes**: VAT, GST, or sales tax
9. **Discounts**: Any applicable reductions
10. **Total Amount Due**: Final amount payable
11. **Payment Terms**: Net 30, Net 60, etc.
12. **Payment Methods**: Bank transfer, credit card, etc.

## Payment Terms
- **Net 30**: Payment due within 30 days
- **Net 60**: Payment due within 60 days
- **Due on Receipt**: Payment due immediately
- **2/10 Net 30**: 2% discount if paid within 10 days, otherwise full amount due in 30 days

## Accounts Receivable
Unpaid invoices become accounts receivable — money owed to your business. Tracking outstanding invoices is critical for cash flow management.

## Invoice Numbering Best Practices
- Use sequential numbering (INV-0001, INV-0002)
- Never reuse invoice numbers
- Consider date-based prefixes (INV-2024-001)
